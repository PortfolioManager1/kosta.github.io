Intro

I've always been an entrepreneur growing up and around the age of 18, I started to teach myself to code. I learned that having the power of creativity and bringing things to fruition, just with the tips of my fingers, motivated me to do more business and projects.

It all began with reselling sneakers and diving into the multi-billion dollar reselling industry. I thought to myself, "how can I acquire multiple pairs of sneakers that were limited and resold on third party markets for double, sometimes triple, the price?". This is where I first started creating 'bots' or request based web applications and analytic tools to keep track of sales and total inventory partnered with proxy generation. Thus, SneakerWare and LinkedProxies were created alongside the gradual increase of my creative solutions and enchanced problem solving skills. By the way, check them out here.

Work

SneakerWare is a private automation e-commerce tool for users to quickly checkout pairs of sneakers from Nike's website. The tool offers 'quick-tasks' , which enables users to create their tasks directly from Nike's SNKRS dashboard.

This allows users to always see 'whats next' without having to worry about constantly checking release dates.

Successfully sold and released 453 beta key licenses through Discord.

Stack: Node.JS / Electron / Bootstrap / JavaScript / MongoDB

LinkedProxies is a dashboard portal for users to purchase proxies for their own respective e-commerce automation tool. Allows users to see their proxy usage through charts and also see recent transactions along with lists of their currently generated proxies.

Being able to monitor proxy bandwidth [ usage ] is crucial for customers since they need to know how much they have left. Users can purchase plans of 2GB all the way up to 10GB worth of bandwidth and can generate as many proxies as they want since they are residential. Residential meaning that they are rotating proxies. Auth0 was used in order to differentiate an admin vs. a customer for authorization purposes.

Stack: Node.JS / ReactJS / JavaScript / Express / MongoDB

Here we see the Admin side of the dashboard. Where admins can facilitate products by adding new ones, disabling them, as well as editing existing products. They can also see all their user checkouts by email [ Checkouts handled by Stripe ]
